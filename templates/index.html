Adimali is a scenic town nestled between Kochi and Munnar in the Idukki district of Kerala, India. Known for its rich history and natural beauty, Adimali became a significant settlement in the mid-20th century. The town is strategically located along National Highway 49, providing easy access to breathtaking destinations like Munnar, Cheeyappara, and Valara waterfalls. The region is famous for its pepper cultivation and is home to key infrastructure projects like Kallarkutti Dam (1961) and Ponmudi Dam (1963), which contribute to Kerala's hydroelectric power. Surrounded by serene villages like Kuthirayalla, Pettimudi, and Maankulam, Adimali serves as a gateway to both Kerala’s lush greenery and Tamil Nadu’s border regions.
